* Strategy Layer Monolith TDD Rules

** 1) Canonical public types
*** Rule 1.1 — The top-level typed container shall be =StrategySpec=.
*** Rule 1.2 — The pair-level typed container shall be =OrderPairSpec=.

*** Rule 1.3 — =OrderSpec= shall be delete immediately and force all callers to build =OrderPairSpec=.

** 2) One ingestion layer for Org strategy tables and run-once

*** Rule 2.1 — Org strategy table ingestion and run-once arguments shall produce the same canonical =StrategySpec= shape before runtime execution.

*** Rule 2.2 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =name=.
*** Rule 2.3 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =window(start_minutes, end_minutes)=.

*** Rule 2.4 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =try_num= as the number of attempts.

*** Rule 2.5 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =dr_pause= as the delay between two attempts.

*** Rule 2.6 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =timeout= as the maximum time allowed for one head to hook.

*** Rule 2.7 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =head(side, order_type)=.

*** Rule 2.8 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =head_price_spec= and =head_price_spec_type=.

*** Rule 2.9 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =head_quantity_spec= and =head_quantity_spec_type=.

*** Rule 2.10 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =tail(side, order_type)=.

*** Rule 2.11 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =tail_price_spec= and =tail_price_spec_type=.

*** Rule 2.12 — The canonical strategy layer shall require =hook_name=.
*** Rule 2.13 — Legacy aliases shall fail at the ingestion boundary; public input uses typed Org fields such as =qty=, =tp=, =pgate=, =hprice=, =oDelta=, =tDelta=, =tUblk=, and =wUblk=.

** 3) Strict invariants

*** Rule 3.1 — The price gate interval =pgate= shall be strictly ascending: =low < high=.

*** Rule 3.2 — Strategy-layer quantities shall be positive.
*** Rule 3.3 — Exchange-specific minimum quantity checks shall be performed at the exchange boundary, not in the strategy parser.
*** Rule 3.4 — String vocabulary shall be normalized once at the parse boundary.

*** Rule 3.5 — The parser shall normalize =side=, order types, trigger codes, and legacy aliases into canonical typed values.
*** Rule 3.6 — Invalid canonical values shall fail fast with deterministic validation errors.

** 4) Enum and value contracts

*** Rule 4.1 — Head lifecycle values shall be explicitly tested.

Allowed =HeadState= values:

#+begin_src text
latent
hooked
submitted
unadmitted
admitted
confirmed
new
living
closed
failed
#+end_src

*** Rule 4.2 — Tail lifecycle values shall be explicitly tested.

Allowed =TailState= values:

#+begin_src text
latent
hooked
submitted
unadmitted
admitted
confirmed
new
living
closed
failed
#+end_src

*** Rule 4.3 — Movement/event values shall not duplicate state values unless they represent a transition event rather than a state.
    LATEN_TO_HOOKED = "latent_to_hooked"
    HOOKED_TO_SUBMITTED = "hooked_to_submitted"
    SUBMITTED_TO_UNADMITTED = "submitted_to_unadmitted"
    SUBMITTED_TO_ADMITTED = "submitted_to_admitted"
    ADMITTED_TO_CONFIRMED = "admitted_to_confirmed"
    CONFIRMED_TO_NEW = "confirmed_to_new"
    CONFIRMED_TO_LIVING = "confirmed_to_living"
    CONFIRMED_TO_CLOSED = "confirmed_to_closed"
    CONFIRMED_TO_FAILED = "confirmed_to_failed"
    NEW_TO_FAILED = "new_to_failed"
    NEW_TO_LIVING = "new_to_living"
    NEW_TO_NEW = "new_to_new"
    NEW_TO_CLOSED = "new_to_closed"
    LIVING_TO_FAILED = "living_to_failed"
    LIVING_TO_LIVING = "living_to_living"
    LIVING_TO_CLOSE = "living_to_close"

*** Rule 4.4 — =HeadState= and =TailState= should be factored into a common =OrderState= unless a state is truly role-specific.
*** Rule 4.5 — Head/tail distinction shall be represented by =OrderRole=, not by duplicating every lifecycle value.

** 5) Behaviour scenarios to freeze

*** Rule 5.1 — The first scenario shall prove that one Org strategy row maps deterministically to one canonical =StrategySpec=.

*** Rule 5.2 — The second scenario shall prove that run-once arguments map deterministically to one canonical =StrategySpec=.

*** Rule 5.3 — The third scenario shall prove that equivalent intent from an Org strategy row and run-once arguments produces the same canonical spec.

*** Rule 5.4 — The fourth scenario shall prove that an invalid interval produces a deterministic validation error.

*** Rule 5.5 — The fifth scenario shall prove that enum contract mismatch fails loudly.

** 6) Error language and operator UX

*** Rule 6.1 — Error messages shall preserve existing user-facing vocabulary.

*** Rule 6.2 — Error messages shall add the precise technical cause.

*** Rule 6.3 — Dry-run mode shall print the canonical parsed structure before any submission command is emitted.
    
*** Rule 6.4 — Dry-run mode shall not execute exchange commands.

** 7) Acceptance gate for implementation

*** Rule 7.1 — Parser contract tests shall pass before the strategy-layer implementation is accepted.
*** Rule 7.2 — Run-once mapping tests shall pass before the strategy-layer implementation is accepted.

*** Rule 7.3 — BDD behaviour scenarios shall pass before the strategy-layer implementation is accepted.

*** Rule 7.4 — Runtime regression tests shall remain green before the strategy-layer implementation is accepted.

*** Rule 7.5 — The implementation phase shall not close while canonical parsing, alias normalization, enum contracts, or dry-run output remain untested.
