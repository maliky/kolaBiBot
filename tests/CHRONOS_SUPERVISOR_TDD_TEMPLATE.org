* Chronos Supervisor Test-Development Rules

** 1. Layer responsibility rules

*** Rule 1.1 — Chronos shall supervise, route, cache, and schedule.

Chronos owns =StrategyState=, receives facts/events, applies ordering/deduplication/precedence rules, and forwards typed events to Isis.

*** Rule 1.2 — Chronos shall not decide lifecycle semantics.

Chronos must not decide whether a head is played, failed, closed, or whether a tail should exist.

*** Rule 1.3 — Isis shall be the only semantic decision layer.

Isis applies:

#+begin_src text
StrategyState + EggMove -> StrategyState + RuntimeCommand(s)
#+end_src

*** Rule 1.4 — Ogun shall be the only command execution layer.

Ogun receives =RuntimeCommand= values and executes them through adapters.

*** Rule 1.5 — Chronos shall not build exchange payloads.

Exchange-specific payload construction belongs to Ogun/adapters, not Chronos.

** 2. Canonical vocabulary rules

*** Rule 2.1 — The strategy container shall be named =StrategySpec=.

*** Rule 2.2 — The pair container shall be named =OrderPairSpec=.

*** Rule 2.3 — The pair runtime state shall be named =PairCycleState=.

*** Rule 2.4 — The strategy runtime state shall be named =StrategyState=.

*** Rule 2.5 — The typed event dataclass shall be named =EggMove=.

*** Rule 2.6 — The typed command value shall be named =RuntimeCommand=.

*** Rule 2.7 — =Chronos= shall mean strategy supervisor.

*** Rule 2.8 — =Ogun= shall mean command executor.

** 3. State cache rules

*** Rule 3.1 — Chronos shall maintain persistent =StrategyState=.

*** Rule 3.2 — The state cache shall include =strategy_id=.

*** Rule 3.3 — The state cache shall include =pair_id=.

*** Rule 3.4 — The state cache shall include =head_client_order_id=.

*** Rule 3.5 — The state cache shall include =tail_client_order_id=.

*** Rule 3.6 — The state cache shall include =head_state=.

*** Rule 3.7 — The state cache shall include =tail_state=.

*** Rule 3.8 — The state cache may include =tail_mode=.

*** Rule 3.9 — The state cache shall include =played_quantity=.

*** Rule 3.10 — The state cache shall include =last_processed_private_event_id=.

*** Rule 3.11 — The state cache shall include =last_processed_private_event_ts=.

*** Rule 3.12 — The state cache shall include =last_emitted_command_id=.

*** Rule 3.13 — The state cache shall include =last_emitted_command_ts=.

** 4. Event ordering and deduplication rules

*** Rule 4.1 — Private terminal events shall win over public price events for the same pair.

*** Rule 4.2 — Duplicate events shall be detected first by =(pair_id, event_id)=.

*** Rule 4.3 — Duplicate events shall fall back to =(order_id, status, ts_bucket)= when =event_id= is unavailable.

*** Rule 4.4 — Stale events shall be ignored.

*** Rule 4.5 — Ignored stale events shall be recorded as =DuplicateEventIgnored=.

*** Rule 4.6 — Events without sufficient identity shall be held in a pending queue.

*** Rule 4.7 — Pending identity events shall fail typed after timeout.

*** Rule 4.8 — Missing identity failure shall not emit exchange commands.

** 5. Command emission rules

*** Rule 5.1 — Command deduplication shall use =(pair_id, semantic_action, client_order_id)=.

*** Rule 5.2 — One loop tick shall keep only the highest-precedence command per pair.

*** Rule 5.3 — Chronos may forward =PlaceHead=.

*** Rule 5.4 — Chronos may forward =AmendHead=.

*** Rule 5.5 — Chronos may forward =CloseHead=.

*** Rule 5.6 — Chronos may forward =FailHead=.

*** Rule 5.7 — Chronos may forward =PlaceTail=.

*** Rule 5.8 — Chronos may forward =AmendTail=.

*** Rule 5.9 — Chronos may forward =CloseTail=.

*** Rule 5.10 — Chronos may forward =FailTail=.

*** Rule 5.11 — Chronos may forward =Ignore=.

*** Rule 5.12 — Chronos shall emit persistence intent instead of performing persistence IO inline.

*** Rule 5.13 — Persistence IO shall be performed by an adapter layer.

** 6. Reducer boundary rules

*** Rule 6.1 — =step_pair= shall own pair lifecycle transitions.

*** Rule 6.2 — =step_strategy= shall own pair routing and strategy-state replacement.

*** Rule 6.3 — Exchange-specific mapping shall stay outside reducers.

*** Rule 6.4 — Reducers shall not read DB.

*** Rule 6.5 — Reducers shall not call REST.

*** Rule 6.6 — Reducers shall not sleep, mutate, or perform IO.

** 7. Tail and chaining rules

*** Rule 7.1 — A tail may be protective.

*** Rule 7.2 — A tail may be an entry tool.

*** Rule 7.3 — A closed tail may trigger a dependent pair.

*** Rule 7.4 — A head may activate tail logic only when its confirmed move is strategically played.

This includes =played_not_canceled= and =played_and_canceled=, even when the played quantity is zero.

*** Rule 7.5 — A failed head shall not activate a tail.

*** Rule 7.6 — The word =closed= shall be preferred over =canceled= in domain vocabulary unless referring to raw exchange terminology.

** 8. Required BDD scenarios

*** Scenario 8.1 — Event routing to one pair

Given three pairs in =StrategyState=.
When a private event for pair B arrives.
Then only pair B state changes.
And emitted commands for pairs A and C are empty.

*** Scenario 8.2 — Duplicate private fill event

Given a duplicated private fill event.
When Chronos processes it twice.
Then Chronos emits commands only once.
And Chronos records the second event as =DuplicateEventIgnored=.

*** Scenario 8.3 — Private terminal event precedence

Given a public price trigger and a private terminal event for the same pair.
When both are seen in the same cycle window.
Then the private terminal event wins.
And the public-trigger event is recorded as ignored.

*** Scenario 8.4 — REST ACK without private confirmation

Given a command ACK without private confirmation.
When timeout expires.
Then pair state remains =admitted=.
And a typed supervision warning event is emitted.

*** Scenario 8.5 — Tail chaining

Given tail chaining is enabled.
When the tail for pair X is =closed=.
Then the hook condition for dependent pair Y becomes eligible.
And Chronos forwards the next typed command according to Isis reducer output.

*** Rule 9.1 — =test_step_strategy_routes_event_to_single_pair= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.2 — =test_step_strategy_preserves_other_pairs= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.3 — =test_chronos_dedupes_duplicate_event= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.4 — =test_chronos_private_terminal_precedence= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.5 — =test_chronos_dedupes_duplicate_command= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.6 — =test_chronos_requires_identity_for_confirmation_match= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.7 — =test_chronos_emits_no_exchange_payloads_directly= shall pass.

*** Rule 9.8 — =test_chronos_emits_typed_runtime_commands_only= shall pass.

** 10. Acceptance gate

*** Rule 10.1 — The supervisor layer shall not be accepted until the state cache schema is fixed and tested.

*** Rule 10.2 — The supervisor layer shall not be accepted until event precedence and event deduplication are tested.

*** Rule 10.3 — The supervisor layer shall not be accepted until command deduplication and command conflict resolution are tested.

*** Rule 10.4 — The supervisor layer shall not be accepted until reducer delegation is tested.

*** Rule 10.5 — The supervisor layer shall not be accepted until at least one chaining scenario is green.

*** Rule 10.6 — No test shall pass by relying on exchange-specific payloads inside Chronos.

*** Rule 10.7 — No test shall pass by allowing Chronos to bypass Isis for lifecycle decisions.
